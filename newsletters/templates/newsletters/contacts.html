about me

I have been working with ceramics for many years. When I first saw the potter at the wheel as a child, I was enchanted! But my steps after high school went in a different direction, to the office. That's when it dawned on me, I don't want to write out tables and make coffee! I want to create mugs that someone else brews coffee into. And so ceramics called me and I listened. And really, in the end I'm sitting here in a dirty apron, dirt behind my fingernails, but I'm right here, this is where I want to be.

The products are either modeled by hand or dialed on a circle, each piece is an original. Nowhere else will you find such a sprite lounging in your garden. Ceramics for the kitchen is not only a practical helper, but also decorates your counter or sideboard. For it. Of course, you can put all ceramics in the dishwasher.

The first mug was actually made from bone. Back in the day (OK, in Neolithic Stone Age), people didnt really know which materials to use to make mugs. Or, you know, they didnt have a lot of options. So, the only reasonable thing to do was to use bone for it.

Although you can use your coffee mug for tea, you should know that a regular coffee mug usually holds about 12 fluid ounces. So basically, you would be drinking two cups of tea at the same time. Hey, we dont judge!

Japan and China gave us our first pottery mugs somewhere in 10,000 BCE.
